Brain-Computer Interface (BCI)

This project aimed to create a portable BCI based on a steady-state visual evoked potentials paradigm using a gtec®; Amplifier and a single board computer. The device was developed to provide patients with severe paralysis a way to control a wheelchair using their electrical brain activity (EEG). The brain's occipital lobe has a natural response to repeated visual stimuli proportional to the stimuli frequency. Different stimuli frequencies lead to different commands to the wheelchair. A customized algorithm needs to be designed to identify each patient brain's response.
The project consisted of 3 stages:

  • Development and implementation of the stimulation modulus
  • Development of the acquisition and processing framework, based on gtec Amplifier APIs
  • Design and implementation of an EEG acquisition, visualization and processing software for the BCI training phase.
Developed using C++ and Matlab. Duration: 12 months.

Laboratorio de Investigación en Neuroimagenología, UAM.

Heart rate measurement app

Development and implementation of the user interface and algorithm for online processing of the electrical heart activity (EKG), acquired by a wireless wearable amplifier. The heart rate is estimated by detecting the R-R Interval and computing its reciprocal. The device was tested in healthy subjects with no Premature heartbeat disorder, and the estimated rate has 95% accuracy compared to measurements done with certified medical devices. Developed using a Texas Instruments microcontroller and Android app development. Duration: 6 months.

Centro Nacional de Investigación en Imagenología e Instrumentación Médica, UAM

Mastography viewer software

Diagnostical Imaging Department, National Cancer Institute. User interface and image processing algorithm to detect the early stages of breast cancer by locating characteristic masses or microcalcifications in mastography images. The software's main features are automated and manual segmentation, volume estimation, 3d reconstruction, simultaneous visualization of the three different planes: Axial, Coronal, & Sagittal. Developed using Matlab and Matlab GUI. Duration: 6 months
